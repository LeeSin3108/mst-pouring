Abstract

The redundancy of multiple perceptual modalities provides reliability for human operation in complex environments. For the first time, we propose an attention-guided multimodal sense action strategy that enables robots to reliably complete manipulation tasks in dynamic and complex perceptual environments.

Our approach develops a Multimodal Sense Transformer(MST) to learn the relevance between tasks and independently encoded modality-specific features across different stages. It adaptively extracts task-critical information from different modalities within noise, then transmits the integrated multisensory features to a Diffusion Model-based action generation head to produce corresponding action sequences for robot execution. With the architecture of MST in multisensory integration, we have achieved visualization of its visual and auditory attention, through which we observe that applying appropriate mask to multimodal perceptions during training enables MST to better learn task-relevant perceptual attention, thereby enhancing the model's operational reliability in complex and dynamic perceptual environments.

Finally, we evaluated our approach on a robot water-pouring task. Experimental results demonstrate that, compared to vision-only methods and simple concatenation of visual-audio features, our method exhibits more reliable task completion capabilities across normal perceptual conditions, visually occluded scenarios, and acoustically interfered environments.

Attention Visualize

No Perception Interference

In normal scenarios without perception interference, MST attention is more focused on visual perception and less on auditory perception.


Visual Occlusion

In the scenarios where visual is obstructed , MST attention is more focused on auditory perception and less on visual perception.


Audio Noise

Under noisy conditions that interfere with hearing, MST allocates attentional resources to the visual domain, while the audio part prioritizes the most behaviorally relevant information.
